## Introduction

South Asia, home to nearly 2 billion people, represents perhaps the most significant untapped retail opportunity of the next decade. While India dominates the narrative, the wider region—including Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nepal, and Sri Lanka—offers complementary opportunities for retail expansion.

## India: The Anchor Market

India's retail story is one of scale, speed, and transformation:

- **Market Size:** India's retail market was valued at approximately $800 billion in 2023 and is projected to reach $2 trillion by 2030.
- **Growth Drivers:** Rapid urbanisation, rising incomes, a young demographic (median age 28), and accelerating digital adoption.
- **Segment Growth:** Electronics, appliances, and modern grocery retail are growing at 15–20% CAGR, outpacing traditional retail categories.

## Key Consumer Trends in India

1. **Digital-First Shopping:** With over 900 million internet users and 700+ million smartphone users, India is one of the world's largest digital markets. E-commerce penetration in electronics exceeds 30%.
2. **Value-Conscious Premiumisation:** While price sensitivity remains high, consumers are increasingly willing to pay for quality, especially in appliances and FMCG.
3. **Tier-2 and Tier-3 Cities:** Growth is no longer concentrated in metros. Cities like Lucknow, Jaipur, Indore, and Coimbatore are driving retail expansion, with lower operating costs and high growth potential.
4. **The Rise of D2C:** Direct-to-consumer brands are proliferating, particularly in electronics, home goods, and specialty FMCG.

## Beyond India: Bangladesh, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka

- **Bangladesh (170M+ population):** Rapid urbanisation and a growing garment industry are fuelling retail growth. Electronics and home appliances are high-growth categories.
- **Pakistan (240M+ population):** A young population and increasing internet penetration are driving e-commerce growth. Electronics and mobile phones account for a significant share of online purchases.
- **Sri Lanka (22M population):** A smaller but sophisticated market with high literacy rates and strong demand for electronics and imported FMCG products.

## Challenges and Considerations

- **Regulatory Complexity:** India's FDI regulations in multi-brand retail remain restrictive. Single-brand retail is more open, with 100% FDI allowed in many categories.
- **Infrastructure Gaps:** Logistics and cold-chain infrastructure are improving but remain uneven, particularly in rural areas.
- **Price Sensitivity:** Consumers in South Asia are highly price-sensitive. Success requires efficient cost structures and value-for-money positioning.
