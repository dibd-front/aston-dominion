## A Market on the Move

Central Asia (led by Kazakhstan) and South Asia (led by India) represent two of the most compelling—yet misunderstood—retail opportunities in the world. These regions are not monoliths; they are diverse tapestries of consumer behaviours, spending patterns, and cultural preferences.

## The Kazakhstan Paradigm

Kazakhstan is the engine of Central Asian retail. With a population of approximately 20 million and a GDP per capita exceeding $13,000 (PPP), the country boasts a growing middle class with significant disposable income. The retail trade turnover is projected to exceed $50 billion in 2026, with annual real growth of 6.5–7.5%.

Key consumer trends include:

- **Digital Adoption:** E-commerce now accounts for 14.3% of all retail trade, with volumes reaching ₸3.8 trillion (~$8 billion) in 2025—growth of over 19% year-on-year.
- **Category Shifts:** Household appliances account for over 10% of all marketplace sales. Smartphones and gadgets dominate online purchases.
- **Urban Concentration:** With over 60% of the population living in urban areas, major cities like Almaty and Astana offer concentrated consumer bases, making logistics and last-mile delivery more efficient than in more dispersed markets.

## The India Paradigm

India is the anchor of the South Asian retail story:

- **Scale:** Over 1.4 billion consumers, with a median age of just 28 years.
- **Market Size:** Retail market projected to reach $2 trillion by 2030.
- **Digital Commerce:** Over 900 million internet users, 700+ million smartphone users.
- **Category Growth:** Electronics and appliances account for over 30% of all e-commerce sales, driven by rising disposable incomes and urbanisation.
- **Tier-2 & Tier-3 Cities:** Beyond the metros, smaller cities are driving growth, with e-commerce penetration expanding rapidly in areas like Lucknow, Jaipur, and Coimbatore.

## The Azerbaijan Opportunity

Azerbaijan's retail sector is characterised by rapid formalisation and a shift toward non-food consumption. In 2025:

- Total retail turnover reached 67.6 billion manats (~$40 billion), growing 3.8% year-on-year.
- Non-food retail grew at 6.8%—almost double the overall rate—driven by electrical appliances, furniture, and textiles.
- The country's strategic location as a transit hub between Europe and Asia offers logistics advantages for import-dependent retail chains.

## Georgia: The Caucasus Hub

Georgia is emerging as a logistics and e-commerce gateway for the wider Caucasus region. Key drivers include:

- High smartphone penetration and expanding internet connectivity.
- A growing trend toward online shopping across home appliances, electronics, and fashion.
- An open business environment with streamlined customs procedures and a favourable tax regime for foreign investors.

## Strategic Implications for Retailers

For international retail chains, these markets offer a "first-mover advantage" window. Unlike Southeast Asia, where competition is fierce, these markets are still relatively under-penetrated by international players. First-movers who enter now can establish brand loyalty and secure prime retail real estate before the market becomes crowded.
