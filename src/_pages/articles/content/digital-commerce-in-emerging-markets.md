## The Omnichannel Imperative

The distinction between "online" and "offline" retail is disappearing across all our markets. Consumers expect seamless integration—order online, pick up in-store; browse in-store, buy via app; return anywhere. Retailers who fail to provide this experience are losing market share.

## Regional Digital Landscapes

- **Southeast Asia:** Super Apps like Grab, GoTo, and Shopee dominate. Retailers must integrate with these platforms while maintaining their own direct-to-consumer channels.
- **Middle East:** Noon and Amazon.ae are the dominant players, but local platforms like Awok (UAE) and Jarir (KSA) maintain strong followings.
- **South Asia:** India's e-commerce market is dominated by Flipkart, Amazon.in, and emerging players like Tata Neu and Reliance's JioMart. The market is characterised by intense price competition and rapid innovation.
- **Kazakhstan:** The marketplace ecosystem is rapidly evolving. Major players include Kaspi.kz (which has evolved from a fintech into a retail powerhouse), Ozon, and Wildberries. Kaspi.kz alone processes a significant share of the country's e-commerce transactions.
- **Azerbaijan & Georgia:** E-commerce is nascent but growing. Local platforms are emerging, and international players are beginning to enter. Logistics and payment infrastructure are improving rapidly.

## The Data Advantage

Our omnichannel solutions leverage data to create a unified view of the customer across all touchpoints. This allows clients to:

- Personalise marketing communications based on purchase history and browsing behaviour.
- Optimise inventory allocation between physical stores and fulfilment centres.
- Reduce return rates through better product descriptions and sizing tools.

## The Logistics Layer

Cross-border logistics for electronics and FMCG in emerging markets is complex. We navigate:

- Customs clearance and import duties (critical for electronics, which often face higher tariffs).
- Last-mile delivery challenges in areas with less developed address systems.
- Returns management, which is often more costly than in mature markets.

Our logistics optimisation reduces lead times and costs, allowing clients to compete effectively with local players who have established infrastructure.
