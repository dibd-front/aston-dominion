## Introduction

The retail landscape across Southeast Asia, the Middle East, South Asia, and the Caucasus/Central Asia is undergoing a fundamental transformation. The traditional binary distinction between "online" and "offline" retail is disappearing, replaced by a seamless, integrated consumer experience. This evolution is not happening uniformly across regions, but the direction of travel is clear: the future of retail is hybrid, omnichannel, and deeply localised.

## What is Driving the Convergence?

### 1. Smartphone Penetration & Digital Literacy

Across all our focus markets, smartphone adoption is surging:

- **Southeast Asia:** Over 400 million smartphone users, with penetration exceeding 70% in urban areas.
- **Middle East:** Smartphone penetration is among the highest globally, exceeding 80% in the UAE and Saudi Arabia.
- **South Asia:** India has over 900 million internet users and 700+ million smartphone users, with the market growing at 10–15% annually.
- **Kazakhstan:** Smartphone penetration exceeds 80%, with most consumers using mobile devices for shopping.
- **Azerbaijan & Georgia:** Rapid mobile adoption is driving e-commerce growth in both markets.

### 2. Changing Consumer Behaviour

Consumers increasingly expect to research products online and purchase in-store (or vice versa). Key behaviours across all markets include:

- **Showrooming:** Testing products in-store, then buying online at a better price.
- **Webrooming:** Researching online, then purchasing in-store for immediate fulfilment.
- **Click-and-Collect:** Ordering online and picking up in-store (or at a nearby collection point).
- **Unified Loyalty:** Expecting loyalty programmes and promotions to work seamlessly across channels.

### 3. The Super App Phenomenon

In Southeast Asia, Super Apps like Grab, GoTo, and Shopee are transforming retail. Consumers can order groceries, electronics, and meals—all within a single app. Similar trends are emerging:

- **South Asia:** Tata Neu, Flipkart, and Amazon.in are creating unified commerce ecosystems.
- **Middle East:** Noon and Talabat are expanding beyond delivery into retail.
- **Kazakhstan:** Kaspi.kz has evolved from a fintech platform into a dominant e-commerce marketplace, offering banking, shopping, and delivery in one app.

### 4. Logistics Infrastructure Investment

Governments and private investors are pouring capital into logistics infrastructure:

- **Southeast Asia:** Rapid development of cold chains and last-mile delivery networks.
- **India:** Massive investment in warehousing, expressways, and rural delivery networks.
- **UAE & Saudi:** Becoming global logistics hubs with world-class port and air freight facilities.
- **Kazakhstan:** Development of e-commerce fulfilment centres and cross-border logistics corridors.

## Regional Deep Dives

### Southeast Asia: The Pioneer of Convergence

Southeast Asia is arguably the most advanced in omnichannel retail integration:

- E-commerce penetration reached 20% of total retail in 2025, up from just 5% in 2018.
- Physical retailers are rapidly digitising, with over 60% of traditional retailers now offering some form of online channel.
- Grocery delivery apps (GrabMart, ShopeeFood, HappyFresh) are blurring the lines between FMCG and e-commerce.

### The Middle East: Luxury Meets Digital

The Middle East presents a unique convergence story:

- Luxury brands are investing heavily in omnichannel experiences—digital showrooms, virtual try-ons, and immersive AR experiences.
- E-commerce penetration is projected to reach 20% by 2028, up from 12% in 2023.
- Modern grocery retail is expanding rapidly, with online grocery delivery gaining significant traction in urban areas.

### South Asia: The Scale Play

South Asia offers unmatched scale:

- India's online grocery market is projected to reach $24 billion by 2027, growing at 25% CAGR.
- Electronics and appliances account for over 30% of all e-commerce sales in India, driven by smartphone and home appliance demand.
- Traditional retail (kirana stores) is digitising through B2B platforms that connect millions of small retailers with FMCG suppliers.

### Central Asia & Caucasus: The Rising Front

- **Kazakhstan:** E-commerce now accounts for 14.3% of retail turnover, with major players like Kaspi.kz and Ozon driving digital adoption. Electronics and appliances account for over 10% of marketplace sales.
- **Azerbaijan:** Online shopping is growing rapidly, particularly for electronics, fashion, and household goods.
- **Georgia:** The e-retail market is expanding, driven by increasing internet penetration and a growing trend of online shopping.
